# Private Marketing Agency Blueprint - What You Walk Away With

About 2.5 hours of focused work and you finish with a complete AI-powered marketing pipeline, working, that you can use for your own business.

---

## What You'll Have Built

- [ ] A `marketing-agency` project folder Claude Code recognises, with a working `CLAUDE.md` it reads at the start of every session
- [ ] An OpenRouter plugin that gives Claude access to image generation models and model discovery through one account
- [ ] A `brand/brand-guidelines.md` document that defines your brand voice, visual identity, audience, and content pillars
- [ ] A content researcher skill that pulls trending topics, competitor angles, and audience questions for any content pillar
- [ ] A Content Calendar table in NocoDB with a month of planned content
- [ ] A content writer skill that produces social posts, articles, and video scripts in your brand voice
- [ ] A content designer skill that generates brand-consistent visuals
- [ ] At least one AI-generated video with an avatar presenter speaking your script
- [ ] A landing page for your business deployed to a live `*.vercel.app` URL
- [ ] A master `/content-engine` command that chains research, planning, writing, and design in one run

---

## What You'll Have Learned

By the end of the course you will have used each of these Claude Code concepts in context, for a real reason:

- **CLAUDE.md** as the standing brief Claude reads every session, loaded with your brand context
- **Slash commands** for triggering your marketing pipeline with one keystroke
- **Skills** for teaching Claude how YOU want research, writing, design, and video done
- **MCPs** for giving Claude access to systems outside your file tree (NocoDB for data, HeyGen for video, Vercel for deployment)
- **Plugins** for extending Claude's capabilities (OpenRouter for image generation)
- **Subagents** for splitting research across focused parallel jobs

You will not just have read about these. You will have used each one for a real purpose and seen what changes when you do.

After this course you will be able to start a new Claude Code project from scratch, on your own, using the same pattern:

- Folder with CLAUDE.md
- Brand context that every agent references
- Skills for each job in your workflow

The next tool you build will take you half the time.

## Three Real Adaptations Covered in Lesson 8

You do not leave the course wondering what to do with what you built. Lesson 8 walks you through three real-world adaptations of the same pattern:

1. **Client onboarding system.** Same agent pipeline, different brand context per client. A freelancer or agency can spin up a content system for each client by swapping the brand guidelines file. Each client gets their own research, calendar, content, and visuals without rebuilding anything.

2. **Product launch campaign.** Focused burst of content around a launch date. The content calendar skill generates countdown content (teaser, announcement, follow-up). The writer produces platform-specific variations. The designer creates a consistent visual series. Everything scheduled to a tight timeline.

3. **Seasonal campaign automation.** Holiday, back-to-school, end-of-year. Plan content months ahead by feeding seasonal hooks into the researcher agent. The calendar fills with timely content. The writer and designer produce batches you schedule in advance.

## What This Course Does NOT Give You

Honest about what is missing, so you know what you are getting:

- A fully automated hands-off system. You still review and approve content before publishing.
- Multi-user team access with permissions and roles.
- Analytics and performance tracking for published content.
- A/B testing workflows to optimise content.
- Production-grade error handling, retries, and monitoring.
- Scheduled auto-publishing to platforms (the publisher formats content for copy-paste, not direct API posting).

The system you build in this course is the foundation. It handles the heavy lifting of research, planning, writing, and design. You handle the judgement calls. That is the right split for a first build.
